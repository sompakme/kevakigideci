In 2004, a brand-new series descended upon the entertainment industry like a storm; everyone was talking about it, due to its surprisingly unique, mystery box-like storytelling.

Of course, the series was Lost, which is now an iconic piece of television history.

Although Lost concluded its run 14 years ago, Lost fans haven't forgotten the complex twists and turns of the show; today, the series is still the talk of the town.

Many viewers regard four-time Emmy winner Lost as the best television show of all time, while others praise the series for transforming modern TV, ushering in a new age of prestige, and helping the medium to rival film in terms of quality.

Of course, very few regard it as overrated (reflexive opposition always makes itself known), but the point is, Lost remains front and center in discussions of television's most groundbreaking series.

Nominated for a staggering 54 Emmys, the concept behind Lost was (in several respects) simple: A passenger jet crashed, and its survivors were marooned on what appeared to be a remote island.

Still, the unexpected sci-fi themes of the story, its fast-paced approach, and Lost's sudden and mysterious plot twists came together, and made the series impeccable.

Lost didn't just make a huge amount of money; it also made stars, and members of the Lost cast who were already famous enhanced their positions in Hollywood as a result of its prominence.

Some of the Lost cast went on to work on new and different projects, and amassed a lot of money due to the fame Lost bestowed upon them.

Let's see how every major member of the Lost cast is doing right now, and look at how much wealth they have amassed 14 years after their career's most successful TV show drew to a controversial close.
