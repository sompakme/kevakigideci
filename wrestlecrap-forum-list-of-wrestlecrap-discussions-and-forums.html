The History of the Wrestlecrap Forum

The Wrestlecrap Forum holds a fascinating history within the pro wrestling community. Originally, the forum was a part of WrestleCrap, a site dedicated to highlighting the worst and most entertaining aspects of professional wrestling. However, in October 2010, the forum split off from WrestleCrap and established itself as an independent entity.

Since its inception, the Wrestlecrap Forum has been a treasure trove of WrestleCrap history. Within its archives, you can find valuable correspondences, including messages from the late John Tenta, a beloved wrestler who contributed to the industry with his unforgettable characters such as Earthquake and Avalanche.

Furthermore, the Wrestlecrap Forum became a part of the Freakin’ Awesome Network (FAN) Forums, an expansive platform covering various aspects of pop culture. This collaboration has enriched the forum’s content, allowing members to explore not only the world of professional wrestling but also other fascinating realms within the fandom community.

The FAN Forums offer a diverse range of discussions, from movies and TV shows to video games and comic books.
